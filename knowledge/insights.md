# OFFICE SPACE

## What Atlanta Businesses Should Know Before Signing an Office Lease
**URL:** https://aravindmurari.github.io/trinity-cre/insights/office-lease-guide-atlanta-tenants
**For:** Office tenants, anyone about to sign or negotiate an office lease
**Date:** May 22, 2026 | Tenant Resources | 5 min

Key content:
- Most businesses approach an office search like renting an apartment — costly mindset for a 5-year commercial lease
- Before touring: document real requirements — workstations today vs. in 2 years, private vs. open, parking (underestimated in Atlanta), conference room capacity
- Full cost: quoted rent (e.g. $28/SF) is just the start — CAM pass-throughs add $10-18/SF; always ask for current year operating expense estimate
- TI allowances: Atlanta market currently $40-80/SF depending on class/landlord/term; buildout costs run $70-120/SF+ — negotiate the gap
- What to negotiate beyond rent: free rent (2-6 months is normal), renewal options, termination rights, exclusivity clauses, parking ratio and cost
- Tenant rep costs tenants nothing — landlord pays the commission at close
- Burke's quote: "The landlord's first offer is a starting position. In an Atlanta office market where vacancies are elevated, tenants who negotiate thoughtfully get significantly better deals than those who do not."

## Atlanta's Office Market in 2026: What Owners Need to Do to Get Leased Up
**URL:** https://aravindmurari.github.io/trinity-cre/insights/office-landlord-leasing-playbook-atlanta-2026
**For:** Office landlords, property owners with vacancy, anyone asking about Atlanta office market conditions
**Date:** May 15, 2026 | Industry Commentary | 5 min

Key content:
- Atlanta office market is bifurcated: Class A in walkable, amenity-rich locations (Midtown, Buckhead, Perimeter) absorbing well; Class B suburban office parks facing structural headwinds
- Post-pandemic, office must compete with WFH — modern HVAC, high-speed internet, natural light, restaurant proximity are now baseline expectations, not differentiators
- If your building can't deliver, you're competing only on price — a race you can only lose so slowly
- Atlanta asking rents 2026: Gwinnett Class B $18-24/SF gross, Class A $24-32/SF; North Fulton slightly higher; Perimeter/Midtown command premiums
- TI allowance is the primary leasing lever — landlords offering strong TI ($75/SF) close more leases; cash-flow-positive within 18 months of occupancy
- Who is leasing office now: professional services (law, accounting, financial advisory), healthcare/medical, tech, small/mid-size businesses outgrowing co-working, government tenants
- Burke's quote: "The landlords winning in today's Atlanta office market are the ones who understand what tenants need before tenants walk through the door."

# INDUSTRIAL

## How to Negotiate Your Industrial Lease in Atlanta: A Tactical Guide for Tenants
**URL:** https://aravindmurari.github.io/trinity-cre/insights/industrial-lease-negotiation-guide-atlanta
**For:** Industrial tenants negotiating or renewing leases, businesses looking for warehouse/distribution space
**Date:** May 8, 2026 | Tenant Resources | 5 min

Key content:
- Most tenants accept the first lease proposal — those who negotiate typically save 10-20% over the lease term
- Start 9-12 months before you need to move — with 90 days to expiry, you lose almost all leverage
- Most effective tool: a competing LOI from another landlord (not a bluff — landlords can tell the difference)
- What to negotiate: base rent (Atlanta range $6.00-10.00/SF NNN depending on submarket), free rent (1-4 months common), annual bumps (standard 3% — try to get 2% or fixed dollar), landlord work (get in writing what HVAC will be serviced and deferred maintenance addressed), renewal options, right of first refusal on adjacent space
- Landlord's broker has done this dozens of times; tenant rep costs nothing (landlord pays commission) and gives you equivalent market knowledge
- Burke's quote: "In the current Atlanta industrial market, tenants have more leverage than the headline vacancy numbers suggest. Use it."

## Selling Your Industrial Building in Atlanta: What Every Owner Should Know
**URL:** https://aravindmurari.github.io/trinity-cre/insights/selling-industrial-building-atlanta-guide
**For:** Industrial building owners considering a sale, owner-users thinking about monetizing real estate
**Date:** April 30, 2026 | Industry Commentary | 6 min

Key content:
- Commercial sales work very differently from residential — different buyers, different diligence, different pricing drivers
- Value drivers for owner-occupied: location, clear height, dock doors, power, truck court depth, building condition
- Value drivers for leased investment: rent relative to market, tenant credit quality, remaining lease term, escalation structure
- Atlanta industrial values are relatively stable post cap rate reset of 2023-2024 — not a distressed market, not a premium market
- Best time to sell depends on your specific situation: maturing debt, near-term lease expiration, significant deferred capex are all signals to act
- Preparation checklist: lease docs and amendments, 3 years of operating expenses, address visible deferred maintenance, confirm zoning, understand your tax position
- Choosing the right buyer pool: owner-users vs. investors evaluate very differently — positioning to the right pool determines price
- Burke's quote: "The sellers who get the best outcomes are not the ones who list and wait. They are the ones who prepare, price correctly, and market to the right buyer pool."

## How to Buy Your First Commercial Investment Property in Greater Atlanta
**URL:** https://aravindmurari.github.io/trinity-cre/insights/buying-commercial-investment-property-atlanta
**For:** First-time commercial investors, anyone asking how to evaluate or buy commercial real estate in Atlanta
**Date:** April 18, 2026 | Investor Guides | 7 min

Key content:
- Commercial investing is different from residential: bigger numbers, business tenants, complex leases, different financing
- Pick one asset class to learn well first — industrial is more compelling starting point: lower opex burden, stronger demand drivers, predictable income
- Cap rate explained: NOI / purchase price; Atlanta industrial running 5.25-6.5% for well-located product; also evaluate cash-on-cash return and IRR
- Buying a commercial property = buying a lease; read the full lease, check tenant credit, understand escalation structure and remaining term
- Due diligence checklist: Phase I ESA (never skip on industrial), commercial inspector (not residential), zoning confirmation, title work, attorney lease abstract
- Financing: 25-35% down, underwritten on property income, 5-7 year terms, 25-30 yr amortization; rates currently 6.5-7.5%; SBA 504 worth understanding for owner-occupants
- Burke's quote: "Your first commercial investment sets your pattern. Approach it with the rigor you would give any significant business decision."

## When to Sell Your Atlanta Commercial Property — And How to Maximize the Price
**URL:** https://aravindmurari.github.io/trinity-cre/insights/when-to-sell-commercial-property-atlanta
**For:** Commercial property owners evaluating an exit, investors asking about timing or sale process
**Date:** April 5, 2026 | Investor Guides | 6 min

Key content:
- The exit is where returns are realized — timing and execution matter as much as the original purchase
- Market is stable post-2023 cap rate reset; waiting for 2021 pricing costs income without delivering upside
- Property-level signals to sell: lease has 3-5 years or less remaining, facing a major capex event, debt maturing into today's rates, significant embedded gain (1031 exchange window), concentration risk
- Preparation: have 3 years of operating statements, current rent roll, lease copies, maintenance records, and environmental reports ready on day one; address visible deferred maintenance before listing
- Overpriced listings sit and develop stigma — price based on actual market comps, not what you want to net
- Best process: targeted to the right buyer pool (owner-user, local investor, regional institutional), not broad-market spray-and-pray
- Burke's quote: "You do not get a second chance at a first offer. The preparation and positioning you do before a property goes to market determines the price you get."

## NNN vs. Gross Leases: A Plain-Language Guide for Industrial Investors
**URL:** https://aravindmurari.github.io/trinity-cre/insights/nnn-vs-gross-leases-industrial-investors
**For:** Anyone asking about NNN leases, lease structures, what NNN means, investment due diligence
**Date:** February 15, 2026 | Investor Guides | 6 min

Key content:
- NNN: tenant pays property taxes, insurance, maintenance/repairs — landlord's only obligation is debt service
- Not all net leases are equal — Modified Gross may leave roof/structure with landlord; always read the actual lease
- Critical items: who pays roof repairs AND replacement, HVAC coverage, annual expense cap, tenant audit rights
- Gross leases: less common in industrial, more common in flex/office-warehouse; landlord absorbs expense volatility
- Burke's quote: "A true NNN lease with a creditworthy tenant and a long remaining term is as close to passive income as commercial real estate gets. But the devil is in the definitions."
