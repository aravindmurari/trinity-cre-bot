# Atlanta Industrial Market — Context & Insights

## Why Atlanta Industrial?

Atlanta is one of the top industrial markets in the Southeast United States. Key drivers:

- **Logistics hub:** Atlanta sits at the intersection of I-75, I-85, I-20, and I-285 — making it ideal for regional and national distribution
- **Hartsfield-Jackson:** The world's busiest airport drives significant air cargo and logistics demand
- **Port of Savannah proximity:** 4-hour drive to the country's fastest-growing container port
- **Population growth:** Metro Atlanta continues to grow, driving last-mile delivery demand
- **Manufacturing base:** Automotive (Kia, Rivian), aerospace, and food/beverage manufacturing anchor industrial demand

## Key Industrial Submarkets in Atlanta

**Northeast Atlanta (I-85 corridor):**
- Norcross, Suwanee, Buford, Braselton
- Mix of Class A and Class B distribution
- Strong demand from e-commerce and logistics

**Northwest Atlanta (I-75 corridor):**
- Marietta, Kennesaw, Acworth, Smyrna
- Established distribution corridor
- Cumberland/Windy Hill submarket is active

**East Atlanta / Conyers:**
- Rail access
- Larger format industrial facilities
- Manufacturing presence

**South Atlanta / Airport:**
- Airport proximity drives air cargo and time-sensitive logistics
- Forest Park, Hapeville, Fairburn

**West Atlanta:**
- Douglasville, Lithia Springs
- Growing submarket with newer Class A spec construction

## Industrial Asset Classes

**Class A:**
- Built within last 10-15 years
- 32'+ clear heights (often 36'-40')
- ESFR sprinklers
- Cross-dock or rear-load configurations
- Modern electrical and trailer parking
- Typically commands premium rents

**Class B:**
- Older vintage but functional
- 24'-30' clear heights
- Dock-high and drive-in loading
- Value play for cost-conscious tenants

**Flex / R&D:**
- Mixed warehouse and office (typically 30-50% office)
- 18'-24' clear heights
- Popular with tech, biotech, light manufacturing
- Often found in suburban business parks

## Common Lease Structures

**NNN (Triple Net):**
- Tenant pays base rent + property taxes + insurance + maintenance
- Most common in industrial leases
- Base rate appears lower but total occupancy cost includes NNN

**Gross / Modified Gross:**
- Landlord covers some or all operating expenses
- Less common in industrial
- More common in flex/office-industrial

**Lease Term:**
- Standard industrial lease: 3-5 years
- Larger deals often 5-10 years
- Shorter terms typically command premium pricing

## Market Dynamics (Current)

- Atlanta industrial vacancy remains historically low
- New spec construction has increased supply but demand continues to absorb it
- Rents have increased significantly since 2020 across all submarkets
- Tenant rep is especially valuable in a tight market — off-market relationships matter
- Sale-leaseback transactions have become more common as owners monetize real estate

## Investment Benchmarks (General Guidance)

- Cap rates for stabilized Class A product: typically 5.0%–6.5%
- Class B cap rates: generally 6.5%–8.0%+
- Cash-on-cash returns vary by leverage and lease terms
- Note: Specific cap rates depend on tenant credit, lease term, location, and market timing — always verify current market data
