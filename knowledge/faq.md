## For Tenants

**Q: I need industrial space but I'm not sure what size I need. Can you help?**
A: Yes. Burke can help you calculate the right square footage based on your operations, inventory volumes, workflow, and growth plans. A site tour of a few options usually clarifies requirements quickly.

**Q: How long does it take to find and lease industrial space in Atlanta?**
A: Typical timeline from starting the search to signing a lease is 60-120 days for most transactions. Larger requirements or build-to-suit situations take longer. Plan for at least 6 months if you need a custom build-out.

**Q: What is NNN rent and what does it mean for my total cost?**
A: NNN (triple net) means the quoted lease rate is base rent only — you also pay your share of property taxes, insurance, and common area maintenance (CAM). For budgeting, add $2-4/SF to the base rate to estimate total occupancy cost, though the exact amount varies by property.

**Q: Do you handle lease renewals, not just new leases?**
A: Yes. Lease renewals are often where tenants leave the most money on the table. Burke can represent you in renewal negotiations to ensure you're getting market terms.

## For Buyers and Investors

**Q: What cap rate should I expect for Atlanta industrial assets?**
A: Cap rates depend heavily on asset class, tenant credit quality, lease term remaining, and submarket. Stabilized Class A product has been trading in the 5.0%-6.5% range. Class B is generally 6.5%-8%+. Burke can provide a more specific range for your target criteria.

**Q: Can you source off-market deals?**
A: Yes. Off-market sourcing is one of the core advantages of working with an experienced broker who has long-standing relationships in the Atlanta industrial market.

**Q: Do you handle 1031 exchanges?**
A: Yes. Burke can help identify replacement properties that meet 1031 exchange requirements and timeline constraints. It is advisable to coordinate with your tax advisor early in the process.

## For Sellers

**Q: How do you determine the value of my industrial property?**
A: Burke conducts a broker price opinion (BPO) based on recent comparable sales, current market cap rates, your property's occupancy and lease terms, and physical condition. This is typically the first step after an initial conversation.

**Q: How long does it take to sell an industrial property in Atlanta?**
A: Well-priced, stabilized properties typically go under contract within 30-60 days of marketing. Vacant properties or those with deferred maintenance take longer. The full process from listing to closing is usually 90-180 days.

**Q: Do you create an offering memorandum?**
A: Yes. Trinity CRE prepares a professional offering memorandum (OM) for listed properties, including financial analysis, property overview, market context, and photos.
